  • Nouns

    Nouns represents persons, places, things, or ideas. Examples include Bill, mountain, cup, and thought.



  • Verbs

    Verbs are action words. Examples include think, went, and breathe.





  • Adjectives

    Adjectives describe nouns. Examples include big, crisp, and nice.





  • Adverbs

    Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs. They usually end in -ly. Examples include quickly, lovely, and quietly.





  • Articles

    Articles modify nouns. English has three: the definite article "the", and the indefinite articles "a" and "an".

    Articles are sometimes classified with other parts of speech. See the complications entry below.

  • Conjunctions

    Conjunctions join words or phrases together. Examples include and, although, and if.





  • Prepositions

    Prepositions are small words that usually describe where, when, why or how something takes place. They are usually followed by a noun phrase. These are called prepositional phrases. Examples include to, at, and in.





  • Pronouns

    Prounouns are small words that replace nouns that have previously been referred to. There are seveal categories of pronouns including:

    • Personal pronouns. Examples include I, you, and they
    • Possessive pronouns. Examples include your, his, and her
    • Demonstrative pronouns. Examples incliude this, that, these, and those.
    • Question words. Well, sometimes, see complications below. Examples inclide who, what, and when.




  • Interjections

    Interjections are short words or phrases that express reactions. Examples include wow, ouch, and hey.
